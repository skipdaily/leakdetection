Emergency Preparation

Know Before Emergency Strikes

Know Your Water Shutoff Valve Location

Every household member should know where the main water shutoff valve is located and how to turn it off in case of a water emergency.

  • Most main water shutoff valves are located where the main water line enters your home.
  • Common locations include near the water meter, in the basement, crawl space, or utility room.
  • To shut off, turn the valve clockwise until it stops (typically a quarter or half turn).
  • Test the valve periodically to ensure it operates smoothly.

Gas Shutoff Knowledge

In the event of a gas leak, knowing how to safely shut off your gas can prevent dangerous situations.

  • The gas meter is typically located on the exterior of your home.
  • The shutoff valve is usually perpendicular to the pipe when gas is flowing.
  • To shut off, use a wrench to turn the valve a quarter turn until it is parallel to the pipe.
  • Important: If you smell gas, evacuate immediately before attempting to shut off the gas.
  • Once gas is shut off, only a qualified professional should turn it back on.

Emergency FAQ

How do I know if I have a gas leak?

Signs of a gas leak include a sulfur or rotten egg smell, hissing sounds near gas lines, dead or dying plants indoors for no apparent reason, a higher than normal gas bill, or physical symptoms like dizziness, nausea, headaches, or fatigue when in your home but feeling better outside.

What should I do if I suspect a water leak but can't see it?

If you suspect a hidden water leak (signs include increased water bills, the sound of running water when no fixtures are in use, mold/mildew, or damp spots), turn off all water-using appliances and check your water meter. If the meter is still moving, you likely have a leak. Call our emergency line for immediate assistance.
